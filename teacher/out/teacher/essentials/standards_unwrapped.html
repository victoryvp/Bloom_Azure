Dissecting the Standard
The Verbs: What should students be doing?
  • Differentiate: to make a distinction; to show what is different.
  • Observe: to regard with attention, especially to see or learn something with the senses.
The Nouns: What concrete words should students know?
  • Energy: What is needed to do work or cause change
  • Mechanical energy:Energy produced by a machine or moving part
  • Sound energy: energy that travels as waves through the air or water and vibrates the ear drum upon contact
  • Electrical energy: energy resulting from the flow of charged particles
  • Light energy:energy that can be sensed by the eye
  • Heat/Thermal energy: Energy that causes a change in temperature between materials
  • Transform: to change from one type of thing into another
Implications for Instruction and Student Misconceptions
  • Students should be given opportunities to describe and observe differences between common forms of energy.
  • Students should understand that forms of energy can be transformed into other forms such as using electrical energy to produce light energy.
  • Students might think that energy is created when objects are turned on. Remind students energy always exists but often transforms from one form to another.
