      What forms of energy were you exploring today?

      We explored light, heat, sound, electrical, and mechanical energy today.

      How many forms of energy did each of the items in Box 1 use or produce?

      Each item in Box 1 produced one form of energy.

      How many forms of energy did each of the photos in the baggie use or produce?

      Each item pictured in the baggie produced all five forms of energy.

      Understand/Skill and Concept

      List one item for each form of energy.

      - Thermal

      Hand warmers, heat wraps

      - Light

      Glow sticks, charged UV beads

      - Mechanical

      Mechanical pencil, wind up toys, pencil sharpener,scissors, egg beater

      - Electrical

      Photo lightening, photo electric outlet, battery

      - Sound

      Comb, straw, ruler, popsicle stick, pop rock candy

      What is the difference between using energy and producing energy?

      Each item must use a form of energy to be able to produce the form of energy it was designed to make. For example, a radio must use electrical energy to produce or make sound energy.

      What forms of energy do the Christmas lights use and produce?

      The Christmas lights use electrical energy to produce light and heat.

      Apply/Skill and Concept

      Which forms of energy seem to be most common??

      Most of the items use electrical energy and produce sound energy.?

      What type of energy does a piano use to produce sound??

      A piano uses mechanical energy to produce sound.?

      What examples can you list that use mechanical energy to produce sound??

      A drum, cymbals, tuning fork, and triangle all use mechanical energy to produce sound.?

      Analyze/Strategic Thinking

      How can we tell the different forms of energy apart?

      We can tell the forms of energy apart based on the properties of the objects we observe. If it creates or makes noise that we can hear, it gives off sound energy. If it feels warm to the touch, it gives off thermal energy. If it lights up, it gives off light energy. If it moves or has moving parts, it is using mechanical energy.

      What can you infer is happening if your toaster is able to toast your bread?

      I can infer that electrical energy is being used to produce thermal energy.

      Can you think of examples where you have been able to see evidence of sound energy?

      Possible answers include: I have seen objects vibrate on a shelf when the TV was too loud. I have seen ripples in water when I stuck a tuning fork in the water. I have seen a window vibrate when a car with loud music drove by.

      Possible answers include: I can prove that energy exists because I can feel it in the form of vibrations from sound or as heat. I can see light and see the way that mechanical energy is in moving parts. I can see a light bulb light up when an electrical circuit is completed.
